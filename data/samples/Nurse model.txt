1. Staff Nurse (Registered Nurse – RN) 
Job Summary 
A Staff Nurse (RN) provides direct patient care, administers medications, monitors patient 
conditions, and coordinates with doctors and healthcare teams to ensure quality treatment. 
Key Responsibilities 
• Assess patient health and record medical history 
• Administer medications and IV therapies 
• Monitor vital signs and report changes 
• Assist doctors during procedures 
• Maintain patient records and documentation 
• Educate patients and families about care plans 
• Ensure infection control and safety protocols 
Qualifications 
• BSc Nursing / GNM 
• Registered with State Nursing Council 
Skills Required 
• Clinical knowledge 
• Communication skills 
• Critical thinking 
• Compassion and empathy 
Work Settings 
• Hospitals 
• Clinics 
• Nursing homes 
2. Licensed Practical Nurse (LPN) / 
Vocational Nurse 
Job Summary 
LPNs provide basic nursing care under the supervision of RNs and doctors, focusing on 
routine patient care and comfort. 
Key Responsibilities 
• Monitor vital signs (BP, temperature, pulse) 
• Assist patients with daily activities (bathing, feeding) 
• Administer basic medications (as permitted) 
• Dress wounds and assist in procedures 
• Maintain hygiene and patient comfort 
• Report patient status to RNs 
Qualifications 
• Diploma in Practical Nursing / GNM equivalent 
Skills Required 
• Basic clinical skills 
• Attention to detail 
• Patient care and support 
Work Settings 
• Hospitals 
• Long-term care facilities 
• Rehabilitation centers

7. Nurse Practitioner (NP) 
Job Summary 
A Nurse Practitioner is an advanced practice nurse who can assess, diagnose, and treat 
patients independently or collaboratively with physicians. In many settings, NPs function 
similarly to primary care providers. 
Key Responsibilities 
• Conduct comprehensive patient assessments 
• Diagnose acute and chronic conditions 
• Prescribe medications (as per regulations) 
• Order and interpret diagnostic tests (lab, imaging) 
• Develop and manage treatment plans 
• Provide preventive care and health education 
• Manage long-term diseases (diabetes, hypertension, etc.) 
Qualifications 
• MSc Nursing / Doctor of Nursing Practice (DNP) 
• NP specialization certification 
• Registered Nurse (RN) license 
Skills Required 
• Advanced clinical decision-making 
• Diagnostic reasoning 
• Patient counseling 
• Leadership and autonomy 
Work Settings 
• Hospitals 
• Primary care clinics 
• Specialty clinics 
• Telehealth platforms

• Manage airway and breathing

• Specialized anesthesia training program 
• CRNA certification (country-specific) 
Skills Required 
• Precision and attention to detail 
• Strong knowledge of pharmacology 
• Crisis management 
• Quick decision-making 
Work Settings 
• Operation theatres 
• Surgical centers 
• Emergency departments

17. ICU Nurse (Intensive Care Unit Nurse) 
Job Summary 
An ICU Nurse provides intensive, round-the-clock care to critically ill patients who require 
constant monitoring and life-support systems. 
Key Responsibilities 
• Monitor vital signs using advanced equipment (ECG, ventilators) 
• Administer IV medications, sedatives, and life-saving drugs 
• Manage ventilators and other life-support systems 
• Perform frequent patient assessments 
• Respond immediately to emergencies (cardiac arrest, organ failure) 
• Maintain detailed patient records 
• Collaborate with intensivists and multidisciplinary teams 
Qualifications 
• BSc Nursing / GNM 
• ICU/critical care certification (preferred) 
Skills Required 
• Critical thinking and rapid decision-making 
• Strong technical and monitoring skills 
• Ability to work under high pressure 
• Attention to detail 
Work Settings 
• ICU (Medical, Surgical, Cardiac, Neonatal) 
• Super-specialty hospitals
