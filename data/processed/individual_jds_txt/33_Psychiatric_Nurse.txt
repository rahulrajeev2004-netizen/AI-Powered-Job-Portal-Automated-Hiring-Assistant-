33. Psychiatric Nurse 
Job Summary 
A Psychiatric Nurse specializes in caring for patients with mental illnesses and psychiatric 
disorders such as schizophrenia, bipolar disorder, depression, and anxiety. They work 
closely with psychiatrists to provide medical and therapeutic care. 
Key Responsibilities 
• Assess patients’ mental health status and behavior 
• Administer psychiatric medications and monitor side effects 
• Develop and implement individualized care plans 
• Assist in psychotherapy and counseling sessions 
• Manage psychiatric emergencies (aggression, self-harm risk) 
• Maintain detailed patient records and progress notes 
• Educate patients and families about mental health conditions 
Qualifications 
• BSc Nursing / GNM 
• Psychiatric/Mental Health Nursing specialization (preferred) 
Skills Required 
• Strong understanding of psychiatric disorders 
• Emotional resilience and patience 
• Crisis intervention skills 
• Excellent communication and observation skills 
Work Settings 
• Psychiatric hospitals 
• Mental health clinics 
• Rehabilitation centers 
• General hospitals (psychiatric units)
