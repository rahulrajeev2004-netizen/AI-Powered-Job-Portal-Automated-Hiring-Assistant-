9. Nurse Anesthetist (CRNA) 
Job Summary 
A Certified Registered Nurse Anesthetist (CRNA) administers anesthesia and provides 
perioperative care before, during, and after surgical procedures. 
Key Responsibilities 
• Evaluate patients before surgery (pre-anesthesia assessment) 
• Administer anesthesia (general, regional, local) 
• Monitor vital signs during surgery 
• Manage airway and breathing 
• Adjust anesthesia levels as needed 
• Provide post-anesthesia care and pain management 
Qualifications 
• MSc Nursing (Anesthesia / Critical Care preferred) 
• Specialized anesthesia training program 
• CRNA certification (country-specific) 
Skills Required 
• Precision and attention to detail 
• Strong knowledge of pharmacology 
• Crisis management 
• Quick decision-making 
Work Settings 
• Operation theatres 
• Surgical centers 
• Emergency departments
